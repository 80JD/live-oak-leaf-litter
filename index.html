How to Use

1 Inspect & Prep

Our leaves arrive clean and ready to use. For maximum safety, we recommend baking at 200°F for 30 minutes or freezing for 48 hours to eliminate any potential hitchhikers.

2 Layer Your Substrate

Spread a generous 1-2 inch layer across your terrarium floor. The leaves will naturally contour to create hiding spots and foraging areas your animals will love.

3 Maintain & Refresh

As the leaves break down (feeding your cleanup crew), simply add fresh litter on top. This mimics nature's forest floor cycle and keeps your bioactive system thriving.

4 Watch Nature Work

Observe your isopods, springtails, and other microfauna flourish as they feed on the decomposing leaves. Your terrarium becomes a self-sustaining ecosystem!

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this safe for all reptiles and amphibians? +

Absolutely! Our Texas Live Oak leaf litter is 100% natural and safe for all reptiles, amphibians, and invertebrates. It's the same type of leaf litter these animals encounter in their natural habitats.

Do I need to sterilize the leaves before use? +

Our leaves are clean and hand-screened, but we recommend baking at 200°F for 30 minutes or freezing for 48 hours as an extra precaution. This ensures no unwanted organisms make it into your setup.

How much leaf litter do I need? +

One gallon typically covers a 20-gallon terrarium with a 1-2 inch layer. For larger setups or thicker coverage, you may want multiple bags. The leaves compress slightly when settled.

How long does the leaf litter last? +

In a bioactive setup, leaves naturally decompose over 3-6 months as your cleanup crew breaks them down. This is healthy and normal! Simply add fresh leaves as needed to maintain your desired depth.

What makes Texas Live Oak special? +

Texas Live Oak leaves are thick, durable, and naturally resistant to mold. They hold up better than many other oak varieties, making them ideal for bioactive terrariums. Plus, they're beautiful!

Can I use this for springtails and isopods? +

Yes! Our leaf litter is an excellent food source for isopods and provides shelter for springtails. It's essential for maintaining healthy cleanup crew populations in bioactive setups.
